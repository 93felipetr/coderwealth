# Introduction to Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity has become a critical topic in the digital age. With the constant increase in online threats, understanding the fundamentals of data protection is essential for anyone who uses the internet.

## What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practices, technologies and processes designed to protect systems, networks and data against digital attacks, damage or unauthorized access.

### Main Pillars of Cybersecurity

1. **Confidentiality**: Ensuring that only authorized people have access to information
2. **Integrity**: Protecting data against unauthorized modifications
3. **Availability**: Ensuring that systems are accessible when needed

## Common Threats

### Phishing
Attempts to obtain sensitive information through deception, usually via email or fake messages.

### Malware
Malicious software including viruses, trojans, ransomware and spyware.

### Social Engineering Attacks
Exploitation of human vulnerabilities instead of technical techniques.

## Best Practices

### 1. Use Strong Passwords
- Minimum of 12 characters
- Combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers and symbols
- Use password managers

### 2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
2FA adds an extra layer of security, requiring a second form of verification beyond the password.

### 3. Keep Software Updated
Regular updates fix known vulnerabilities that hackers can exploit.

### 4. Be Cautious with Links and Attachments
Always verify the source before clicking links or opening suspicious attachments.

## Essential Tools

### Password Managers
- Bitwarden (Open Source)
- KeePassXC (Free)
- 1Password (Premium)

### Antivirus and Anti-malware
- Malwarebytes
- Bitdefender
- ESET

### VPN for Privacy
- Mullvad VPN
- ProtonVPN
- ExpressVPN

## Conclusion

Cybersecurity is not a destination, but a continuous journey. Staying informed about the latest threats and adopting good security practices is fundamental to protect your data and online privacy.

Remember: security starts with you. Be proactive in protecting your digital information.
